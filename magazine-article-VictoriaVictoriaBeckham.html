Victoria Beckham's charm offensive is working. After winning over the notoriously hard-to-please fashion world with the namesake luxury label she founded in 2008, and two British Fashion awards in the process; she repackaged her impeccable, and innate, sense of style, sense of humour and affinity for hard work for a whole new audience. Four years ago Mrs Beckham launched Victoria Victoria Beckham - the more playful, wittier younger sister line, designed with the same compelling simplicity.

All in all, it's a clever and covetable offering of wardrobe heroes that you'll reach for time and time again, just like Beckham does herself. She's the line's best spokesmodel after all, road-testing her designs at every opportunity. We're extremely excited to be part of the VVB gang beside Mrs. Beckham and rising starlets like Saoirse Ronan and Lily James.

As we find the collections of AW15 bathed in the warming glow of retro nostalgia, an unexpected and compelling protagonist emerges as the season's lead: Margot Tenenbaum, or more accurately, Mr. Wes Anderson himself.

The accomplished director stakes claim to a certain kind of dreamy, surrealist and rather intimate rendering of the 1960s & 70s through all his artistic motion pictures. The Anderson brand of drama, theatre, showmanship: its runway gold. And as for these two eras, the hottest of decades right now, you couldn't find a more fitting scene-setter than Mr. Wes Anderson himself; cue the pine panelling, sunken leather seating, kitsch accessories and all...
