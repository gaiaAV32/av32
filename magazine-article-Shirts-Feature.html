The shirt is always ripe for reinvention. The traditional t-shape can be tweaked, and the proportions pushed, but the seductive simplicity it projects powerfully perseveres. Designers have really rolled up their sleeves when constructing, and deconstructing, their latest versions with plenty of new pulse-quickening details. Check them out below.

Philosophy di Lorenzo Serafini ruffled the shoulders, cuffed the sleeves and added a bowed neck tie underneath the frilled collar of his sheer shirt to nail a naughty-but-nice mood.

Jill Stuart bypassed buttons completely in favour of ruffles running around the neck and wrists. These more relaxed, smocked styles are full of feminine flair: plain or floral.

Rosetta Getty patterned her statement shirting with a punchy paisley print and elegantly lengthened the body to create a tunic. It’s a chic trouser topper matched or clashed.

J JS.Lee cleverly applied graphic coloured lines to her cropped cuts and voluminous sleeves. Those bold horizontal and vertical strokes are strategically placed to create a form-flattering optical illusion.

Jourden’s classic shirt has a tiered skirt which makes the boy-meets-girl balance more overt. The dress’ newfound romance is tempered by the sharp collar and cuffs.
