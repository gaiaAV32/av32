Julie Paskal’s switch from architecture to fashion is clear when admiring the decorative minimalism of her structured styles. Shape-sensitive from the distinctive way she laser-cuts her fabrics, a signature, to the precise tailoring of her fits; the now mother of two launched her label in 2011 and presents collections in Kiev and Paris. Paskal was shortlisted for the 2014 LVMH prize and taps progressively into SS16’S romantic mood via the playful arrangement of layered and cut-out circles.

Lilia Litkovskaya has been making clothes since childhood so launching her award-winning label in 2009, although she began showing at Kiev Fashion Week in 2006, was a natural career move. The duality of her directional designs set them apart: they’re feminine yet masculine, smart yet effortless and exude a captivating sense of urban sophistication. There’s also an intriguing interplay between the contrasting textiles, premium craftsmanship and strong, streamlined silhouettes, explored through the experimental use of sequins and lace this season.

Model turned designer Anna Karenina has used the social media boom to her advantage since launching her witty womenswear label in 2012 aged 16. By keeping her flattering shapes simple, her fairy tale inspirations and imaginative embellishments can shine physically and digitally. She won the 2014 Vogue Italia’s Design.It contest and the reoccurring bow details, in cheerful colours, and the liberal use of pink illustrate the irresistible girlish glamour Anna K’s pieces project. Her online world is followed by 42.1K instragrammers alone.
